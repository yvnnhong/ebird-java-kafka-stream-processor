### Analysis Summary:

**Population Stability Assessment for Streaming Pipeline:**

**Key Findings:**

1. **Consistent Growth Pattern**: 
   - Steady increase from 40,866 birds (2005) to 1,198,934 birds (2024)
   - Represents increased eBird participation rather than actual population explosion
   - Growth rate is predictable and consistent with no wild fluctuations

2. **Stable Geographic Distribution**:
   - Los Angeles County consistently dominates every year
   - San Diego County consistently ranks second
   - Orange, Kern, Sacramento regularly appear in top 5 counties
   - Geographic hotspots remain stable across 20 years

3. **Predictable County Rankings**:
   - Same counties appear in top positions year after year
   - Relative proportions between counties stay consistent
   - No dramatic geographic shifts or habitat abandonment

4. **Population Stability Indicators**:
   - No year-over-year population crashes
   - No dramatic spikes suggesting data quality issues
   - Smooth, predictable growth trajectory suitable for baseline modeling

**Validation Metrics for Synthetic Data Generation:**

- Excellent baseline data: 20 years of consistent observation patterns
- Predictable geographic clustering: LA/San Diego primary hotspots  
- High observation volume: 1M+ recent observations for robust training data
- Low coefficient of variation: Consistent relative distributions between counties
- Stable seasonal patterns: Ready for breeding phenology analysis

**Conclusion:** 

Mourning Dove demonstrates ideal stability characteristics for the streaming anomaly detection system. The species shows predictable, learnable patterns optimal for synthetic data generation and statistical baseline establishment. The consistent geographic distribution and stable population trends over two decades validate this species as an excellent candidate for the real-time bird observation processing pipeline.
